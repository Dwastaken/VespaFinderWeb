Asian Hornet

The Asian hornet (Vespa velutina) is an invasive species that has been found in the Netherlands for several years now. The hornet is harmful to honeybees, bumblebee species, and other pollinating insects, thereby threatening the ecosystem and our environment. For fruit growers, for example, it can be catastrophic if pollinating insects are killed in large numbers. Therefore, nests must be located. An effective method for this is to attach a small transmitter to the hornet. Robor Nature recently developed a revolutionary system, in several respects, to track hornets using a micro-transmitter. Thanks to modern radio technology, targeted searching is possible using compact directional receivers. The transmitters can also be tracked via a smartphone and report their location through the mobile network, making the search easier.

    • The transmitter

    The transmitter itself is extremely lightweight (160 mg) and has a shorter antenna than was previously possible. As a result, the hornet experiences less weight and drag, increasing the likelihood that the hornet with the transmitter can be recaptured after locating the nest. This allows the transmitter to be reused multiple times. The transmitter is rechargeable and can be configured using an included controller. A new and much easier attachment method has been developed, which does not require the hornet to be anesthetized. The attachment process takes only 5 seconds. This means less stress for the hornet, and anyone can safely perform the attachment themselves. The hornet can be released immediately and does not need time to recover and regain strength before being able to fly again.

    Install transmitter

    With the help of specially designed tools, the transmitter can be easily attached to the Asian hornet. All the necessary tools can be found in the AH Tracking Kit.

    Search with Hornet app

    When near the nest, anyone with an Android or iOS smartphone can help locate it using a special app called Vespa Finder. When the transmitter is detected through the app, all other users will also see the detected transmitter on their app. This way, a group of app users can quickly track down an Asian hornet nest. You can do this on foot, by bicycle, or in combination with the ultra-sensitive receivers mounted on a car.

    Search with arrow receiver

    The receiver consists of a compact directional antenna with a smart receiver. You can easily track the signal using the antenna. The signal strength is indicated by 16 LEDs and by variations in tone pitch.

    Search with drone (optional)

    A special receiver has been developed that can be connected to a drone, allowing the signal strength to be displayed on the remote controller. This offers a major advantage, as sensitivity and range are significantly increased when searching from greater heights. The range can be extended up to 2 kilometers.
